Living Room

The living room is often considered the heart of the home. It's a space for relaxation, socializing, and entertainment. Living rooms usually feature comfortable seating arrangements such as sofas, armchairs, and coffee tables. They may also include entertainment centers, bookshelves, and decorative elements like artwork and rugs.

Bedroom

The bedroom is a private sanctuary where individuals retreat for rest and relaxation. It typically contains a bed, nightstands, dressers, and closets for storage. Bedrooms often reflect the personal style and preferences of the occupants, with elements such as bedding, curtains, and lighting contributing to the overall ambiance.

Kitchen

The kitchen is the heart of the home where meals are prepared, and families gather. It typically features countertops, cabinets, appliances, and a sink. Modern kitchens may include islands or breakfast bars for additional workspace and seating. The design of the kitchen focuses on functionality, efficiency, and aesthetics.

Bathroom

Bathrooms are essential spaces for personal hygiene and grooming. They usually consist of a toilet, sink, and bathtub or shower. Bathroom designs prioritize functionality, comfort, and cleanliness. Features such as tile flooring, vanity cabinets, and mirrors contribute to the overall style and functionality of the space.
